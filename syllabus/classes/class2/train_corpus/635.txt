Bangs said although the number of elk has not declined very much since the wolves came back, their behavior appears markedly different. "I'm pretty suspicious of making broad generalizations," however, he cautioned. "Wolves are still pretty new. They've only been there for 10 years. In 10 more years we'll have a better idea." Wolf reintroduction in the northern Rockies has been so successful the Fish and Wildlife Service has moved to reduce the animal's status from endangered to threatened and, eventually, to remove it from the endangered species list altogether. Bangs said the agency has established viable wolf packs in the northern Rockies, as required by federal regulations.
