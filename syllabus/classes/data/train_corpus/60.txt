LTTE arms-running In 1997, the Thai navy reported the interception of a 16-meter boat after a chase off the Thai port of Ranong, and the confiscation of two tons of weapons and ammunition. Among the weapons intercepted were two rocket-propelled-grenade launchers, 20 assault rifles, M-79 grenade launchers and more than 10,000 rounds of ammunition. Four persons were arrested, reportedly belonging to the Manipur Revolutionary People's Front. Six crew members were from the Arakan region of Myanmar. The boat was heading toward Cox's Bazar in Bangladesh. Until 1995 the LTTE maintained a base at Twante, an island off the coat of Myanmar, west of the Andaman islands. Subsequently, Phuket became the LTTE's main backup base. A Sri Lanka-born Tamil with a Norwegian passport was arrested by Thai authorities in 2000 for his links with the LTTE.
